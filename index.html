Summary

This summer was the first summer in seven years that I stayed home. The past few years I went to summer camp - usually as a camper - but the last time I went, I was a counselor. However, even though this summer was unlike my previous ones, it didn't mean I didn't have fun. I spent this summer with my parents. My mom and I went to the beach a lot and spent a ton of time together. Towards the middle of August, I went to visit my grandfather in Brooklyn and I stayed there for a few days. During my stay, I met up with friends in the area and attended a festival. Once I got back from Brooklyn, my friends and I went on a camping trip in Pennyslvania for a weekend before school started up again.

Pier Village

Pier Village, located along the Jersey Shore, offers upscale shopping, dining, entertainment, and residential options. It features a beautiful boardwalk with Atlantic Ocean views and a mix of boutique shops, restaurants, cafes, and cultural events.

Brooklyn Weekend

The annual Festa di Santa Rosalia, also known as the 18th Avenue Feast, is a cultural and religious celebration in Bensonhurst, Brooklyn, honoring the patron saint of Palermo, Sicily, with a lively procession, religious ceremonies, and traditional Italian food.

Camping Trip

Tobyhanna State Park in Pennsylvania offers a serene camping experience surrounded by greenery and 170-acre Tobyhanna Lake. With tent and RV sites, amenities, and recreational activities like hiking, fishing, boating, and swimming, visitors can fully immerse themselves in nature.
